# Commercial Analysis Report

## Objective

The objective of this analysis is to understand the drivers of revenue and customer value in order to support commercial decision making.

---

# Key Insights

## 1. Revenue Concentration

Classic Cars represent the largest share of revenue.

This suggests that this product category is the company's main commercial driver.

---

## 2. Customer Segmentation

The RFM segmentation shows that a relatively small group of **Champion customers** generates a large portion of revenue.

These customers purchase frequently, recently, and spend more money.

---

## 3. Revenue Distribution by Customer Segments

Champion and Loyal customers account for the majority of total revenue.

Customers classified as **At Risk** previously generated significant revenue but have recently reduced their activity.

---

## 4. Product Performance

Classic Cars dominate sales performance across revenue and order volume.

Lower-performing product lines such as Trains contribute a very small percentage of revenue.

---

## 5. Seasonality

Sales show strong seasonality, with peaks in the last quarter of the year.

This suggests a strong influence of holiday demand.

---

# Business Recommendations

## 1. Retention Strategy for Champions

Champion customers represent the most valuable segment.

Recommended actions:

- Loyalty programs
- Exclusive promotions
- Early product access

---

## 2. Reactivation Campaigns for At-Risk Customers

Customers classified as "At Risk" previously generated revenue but have stopped purchasing recently.

Recommended actions:

- Targeted email campaigns
- Personalized offers
- Limited-time discounts

---

## 3. Focus on High-Performing Product Lines

Since Classic Cars generate the highest revenue share, the company could:

- Expand this product line
- Increase inventory
- Promote related products

---

## 4. Optimize Marketing Around Seasonality

Sales increase significantly near the end of the year.

Recommended actions:

- Increase marketing budget before Q4
- Launch seasonal promotions
- Prepare inventory earlier

---

# Limitations of the Analysis

This analysis has several limitations:

1. The dataset covers only **two years and five months**, which may not fully represent long-term trends.

2. The dataset does not include **marketing data**, preventing analysis of campaign performance.

3. Customer acquisition channels are not available.

4. Profit margins are not included, so revenue does not necessarily reflect profitability.

5. Order cancellations represent a small portion of the dataset but could impact revenue estimates.

---

# Conclusion

The analysis shows that revenue is concentrated among specific customer segments and product lines.

By focusing on customer retention strategies, high-performing product categories, and seasonal demand patterns, the company can potentially increase revenue and improve commercial performance.
